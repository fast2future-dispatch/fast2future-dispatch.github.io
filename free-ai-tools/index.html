The one rule before any tool

Free tools make two things easy: building something genuinely useful, and building something that gets you banned. The line is simple — everything here is for ATTRACTING people who opt in, never for scraping or cold-blasting strangers. The same email platform that runs a clean opt-in list can also be misused to spam a bought list; only one of those keeps your account and stays legal. We use the first kind. How the pieces fit (the attraction engine)

These tools aren't a random pile — each one does one job in the same four-part engine:

  1. Attract: AI chat tool drafts a genuinely useful article or guide; you publish it on a free page.
  2. Capture: the email tool's opt-in form collects only people who say "yes."
  3. Follow up: the automation tool fires an AI-drafted welcome sequence the moment someone opts in.
  4. Offer: once you've been useful, you make one honest offer — and disclose any affiliate links.

What to skip (the paid traps beginners fall for)

  • "Lead scraper" tools that harvest contacts — these violate platform terms and privacy law. Skip entirely; they're the opposite of this approach.
  • Expensive all-in-one suites before you've validated anything. Start free, prove the system works, then consolidate if it helps.
  • Five tools at once. Pick one per job. More tools = more to maintain, not more leads.

Your free-stack starter checklist

  1. Pick one email tool (free tier) and create one opt-in form.
  2. Pick one automation tool to connect form → list → welcome email.
  3. Use a free AI chat tool to draft your lead magnet + 3-email welcome.
  4. Put the opt-in on a free one-page site.
  5. Publish one genuinely useful article that answers a buyer's real question, and link to your opt-in.

Why "salesy" outreach backfires for coaches

Cold DMs and pitch-first funnels feel gross because they are — they ask before they give. Your buyers can sense it, and it cheapens the expertise you're actually selling. The coaches who fill their practice do the opposite: they share something genuinely useful, attract the right people, and let trust do the selling.

The attraction system for coaches

  1. Attract: AI helps you answer the exact question your ideal client is stuck on — "how do I [their goal] without [their obstacle]." Publish one genuinely helpful piece.
  2. Capture: offer a focused free resource (an assessment or mini-guide) behind a simple opt-in. One clear promise, one yes.
  3. Follow up: a warm, helpful welcome sequence — deliver the resource, share one real insight, then invite a no-pressure conversation when they're ready.
  4. Offer: by the time you mention working together, you've already helped — so it's a natural next step, not a pitch.
Keep it honest: never fake testimonials or results to pull opt-ins. Your real client outcomes (with permission) and genuine insight beat invented hype — and they keep you compliant.

Start this week

  • Pick the one question your best-fit client always asks.
  • Have AI draft a short guide or self-assessment answering it; add your expertise.
  • Put it behind a free opt-in; set one warm welcome email.
  • Share it where your people already are — helpfully, not spammily.
